Welcome to my page, Ryan's Ramblings!

I am Ryan Hendricks, and I have more than 10 years of experience in training, education, and enablement within the information technology and cyber security industry. I have the privilege to do what I love. I get to spend my time developing technical training content, nerding out in labs, breaking and fixing everything to find out how it really works, and build training content based on my adventures.

When I transitioned into the Information Technology world, I didn't expect to be this excited about it. One could say that my inner nerd has shown itself. Since then, I have delved deep into the topics of web development, programming, networking, and most importantly cyber security. I pass all knowledge I can to those who want it.

I believe what makes a great educator is not the knowledge they have, but whether they can pass that knowledge onto others. It is much harder than regurgitating information in the hopes it sticks. You must relate it and make it applicable to their lives and experiences all through stories and analogies to help them retain.

My philosophy on instructional design

The field of instructional design is an interesting one. Throughout my years as a trainer, I now appreciate that there is more to creating impactful educational content than putting what you know on paper or in slides and expecting it to work. I continue to build my experience and knowledge in the field. As I do, I find critical aspects of instructional design focus around the a few concepts. Specifically, as it applies in the corporate world where multiple apects impact the process.

Instructional Design Concepts

Building content for the needs of the learner

Designing learning based on the requirements of the business

Using technology to enhance the learning experience

Developing a memorable learning experience
